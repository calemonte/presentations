DNP Projects on ScholarlyCommons
Benefits of having work on SC

Increased visibility & impact

Student's work will be openly accessible to researchers around the world and showcased alongside other members of the Penn community. OA works are known to receive more citations and greater impact.

Permanent links

ScholarlyCommons provides permanent URLs (no dead links!) for each work, meaning students can reliably link to their project on resumes, websites, etc.

"Proof" of scholarly discovery

Having your work online serves as a time-stamped indication of your claim to a scholarly idea, reducing likelihood of getting "scooped".

Participation in showcase of DNP scholarship

Participation in the collection demonstrates quality of work and sits alongside other scholars who have graduated from the DNP program.

Disadvantages of having work on SC

Requires slightly more overhead when publishing

Some publishers may not accept work that has been posted on a preprint server or IR. Students should look into publisher policies related to preprint sharing prior to submission, or embargo work for 1-3 years.

Some students prefer privacy

There are some students who prefer to not distribute their work publicly for one reason or another. This sentiment varies from discipline to discipline. Having a flexible posting policy -- with opt-ins or embargoes -- is a good idea if this is a concern.
